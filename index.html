Additional Condition Concerns Affecting Value

Three items identified during the on-site inspection warrant specific downward adjustments beyond the standard mid-rehab discount. Each represents either a capital repair the next buyer will price in, or an ongoing carrying cost that weakens marketability.

Well Water Quality — 5-Chamber Filtration System

The property is served by a private well that requires a 5-chamber whole-house water filtration system to treat the incoming water, and the current owners test the water annually to monitor quality. The presence of a multi-stage treatment train signals a known water-quality issue — commonly iron/manganese, hardness, arsenic, uranium, or radon, all of which are well documented in Southern Maine groundwater. For the next buyer this means (a) a non-negotiable ongoing maintenance obligation (filter media changes, UV lamps, annual lab testing), (b) replacement of chambers or media on a 5–10 year cycle, and (c) disclosure that will deter water-sensitive buyers and narrow the buyer pool. Appraisers and lenders in Maine routinely require a passing water test at closing, so any deterioration between the current test and a future listing is a live deal risk. This condition supports a measurable downward adjustment against every finished comp, none of which carry the same ongoing burden.

Exterior Siding — Aged Wood Shingle

The Windham tax card lists the exterior as wood shingle, original to the 1975 build. At 51 years old, the siding is at or beyond the typical useful life for untreated or lightly-maintained wood shingle in a Maine climate. Visible weathering, cupping, and spot rot were observed during the walkthrough. In this comp set, every comparable home presents with newer vinyl, fiber-cement, or recently-refreshed exterior cladding, so the subject's siding is a clear inferior feature. A partial repair-and-repaint is a short-term fix; a full re-side to fiber-cement or vinyl is the likely market expectation for a turnkey sale. Either approach is a meaningful out-of-pocket cost the next buyer will net out of their offer.

Driveway — Surface Damage

The driveway shows significant surface cracking, frost heave, and edge failure. In a Maine market where comps present freshly paved or intact gravel/asphalt drives, a damaged driveway is an immediate curb-appeal hit and one of the first items flagged by showing buyers. Rehab options range from an overlay/seal-coat (low-end) to a full tear-out and repave (high-end); either way the work is priced directly into the buyer's offer or surfaces at inspection as a renegotiation point. This item is not covered by the existing rehab lines and warrants its own adjustment across all comps.

The $7,500 adjustment reflects the estimated cost of physical removal and restoration, but does not account for potential buyer reluctance or lender complications which could further impact marketability. Any potential value from preserving the duplex use (if compliance could be documented) has not been attributed here.

Market Context for Rehab Properties

The Windham market shows healthy demand for finished homes, with comparable properties selling in an average of 15 days and achieving a 96% sale-to-list ratio. However, the subject's mid-rehab condition positions it in a different buyer pool — primarily investors and renovation buyers who factor in rehab costs and a profit margin. These buyers typically seek 20–30% below after-repair value (ARV). With comparable finished homes selling between $380,000 and $540,000, and estimated rehab costs of $67,500–$130,000, the subject's current as-is value must account for both the cost to complete and the risk premium demanded by this buyer segment.

Windham Growth Trends

Windham continues to be one of the most desirable suburban communities in Greater Portland. The town's population growth, expanding commercial corridor along Route 302, excellent school system (RSU 14), and proximity to Sebago Lake recreation make it a strong long-term investment. Properties in good condition are selling quickly, and the overall market trajectory supports the thesis that a fully renovated version of this home would compete favorably with the comparable sales analyzed here.
